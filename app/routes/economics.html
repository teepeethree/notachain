Economics v0.3

Notachain Economics

Working Paper — Draft for Discussion — Companion to the Notachain Whitepaper

1. Introduction

The Notachain token ($NTC) is the native token of the Notachain Protocol. It is deployed on an EVM-compatible chain as a standard ERC-20 token.

$NTC is designed to do three things: bootstrap the network by incentivising early attestor registration, create an immediate economic penalty for fraudulent attestation that complements the slower machinery of legal accountability, and generate revenue for wallet holders and attestors through the read-fee mechanism.

This document assumes familiarity with the Notachain whitepaper, which covers the protocol architecture, trust model, and attestation layer.

2. Design Principles

  • Incentivise participation, not idleness. Emissions flow to participants who create value — primarily attestors who register and actively sign attestations.
  • Algorithmic and verifiable. Emission and burn rates are determined on-chain by network activity. No human intervention required.
  • Self-correcting. Low activity triggers higher emissions to stimulate growth. High activity triggers lower emissions and increased burns to reward holders.
  • Accountable treasury. Every release is published with a reason, hashed on-chain, and emitted as a public event. No silent releases. No hidden movements.
  • Instant consequences. Staking creates an immediate economic penalty in seconds. Legal liability follows over months to years. Both are required.
  • Attestations earn. Wallet holders and attestors earn $NTC every time a protocol reads an attestation. The read fee aligns incentives, funds the ecosystem, and protects privacy.
  • Published and auditable. All parameters — curve values, treasury caps, staking thresholds, fee structures — are on-chain.

3. Core Function

$NTC serves four functions within the protocol:

  1. Staking and accountability. Attestors stake $NTC against their registration. The stake is slashable for proven fraudulent attestation, creating an immediate economic penalty that operates in seconds.
  2. Network bootstrapping. Grants denominated in $NTC incentivise early registration of individual legal professionals. A separate grant pool incentivises institutional participants — licensing bodies, insurance companies, and other stakeholders — to join the DAO.
  3. Fee medium and burn. Attestation creation fees and read fees are collected in $NTC. A portion of each fee is permanently burned, creating deflationary pressure proportional to network usage.
  4. Read-fee settlement. $NTC is the medium through which protocols pay for attestation access and through which wallet holders and attestors receive their share of read revenue.

$NTC is not a governance token in the conventional sense. The reference list of recognised licensing bodies is initially maintained by the protocol entity based on publicly verifiable institutional credentials. As institutional participants join the DAO through token grants, governance progressively decentralises. The protocol entity retains operational authority for time-sensitive decisions but is accountable to the DAO through the on-chain audit trail.

4.1 Treasury Governance

Every action against the treasury — whether an authorisation, movement, revocation, or cap adjustment — follows the standard authorisation process:

  1. A reason is published.
  2. A hash of the reason is recorded on-chain.
  3. A public event is emitted.

This process does not change, even if other rules in this document are updated. Authorisation and movement are separate events. Authorised tokens that have not yet been moved carry over between quarters. Authorisations may be revoked if the need no longer exists.

4.2 Spending Cap

The treasury enforces a global quarterly spending cap, adjustable via the standard authorisation process. The full history of cap changes is auditable via contract events.

4.3 Team Vesting

Equal quarterly instalments over two years from launch. Vested tokens leave the treasury permanently. Fixed and not subject to acceleration.

4.4 Budget Reversions

Unspent operational budget tokens revert to the treasury each quarter — a soft deflationary mechanism.

5. Emission and Burn Model

5.1 The Inverse Sigmoid Emission Curve

The emission rate follows an inverse sigmoid as a function of the Network Activity Metric (M) — a 90-day rolling average of attestation volume on the Notachain Protocol. The 90-day window smooths short-term volatility and resists manipulation.

  • Low activity: emission rate at ceiling. Maximum incentives for early participants.
  • Transition zone: the steepest part of the curve. Most responsive to volume changes.
  • High activity: emission rate approaches floor. Minimal new issuance. Network self-sustaining.

Mathematical form: I = floor + (ceiling − floor) / (1 + e^(k × (M − midpoint)))

Where k controls the steepness of the transition and midpoint defines what "healthy attestation volume" means for the protocol.

5.2 The Linear Burn

Burns are linear with activity. Both attestation creation fees and read fees contribute to the burn. The fees serve as a natural defence against attestation farming — emissions must exceed the fee cost for gaming to be profitable.

5.3 Interaction

Both mechanisms operate simultaneously and amplify each other:

  • Low volume: high emissions, low burns. Growth mode. Net inflationary.
  • Crossover point: emissions equal burns. Supply stable.
  • High volume: low emissions, high burns. Deflationary. Network self-sustaining.

5.4 Guardrails

  • Emission ceiling: the emission rate can never exceed the ceiling, regardless of how low activity falls. Prevents runaway inflation during prolonged downturns.
  • Emission floor: the emission rate never drops below the floor. Ensures some minimum incentive always exists.
  • 90-day rolling average: prevents short-term volume spikes from manipulating the emission rate.
  • Treasury awareness: treasury releases during net inflationary periods face a higher bar for justification.

6. Staking Architecture

6.1 Two Penalty Timescales

Mechanism Timescale Severity
Token slashing Seconds Economic loss of staked $NTC
Legal accountability Months to years Criminal prosecution, loss of license, unlimited personal liability

Independent and reinforcing. Staking without legal liability is just another DeFi slashing mechanism. Legal liability without staking leaves a gap during the months before proceedings conclude. Both are required.

6.2 Staking Grants

Early attestors receive their stake as part of their registration grant. However, the grant is staked directly into the registry contract on their behalf — it is not sent to their wallet. The attestor cannot sell or transfer the staked tokens.

The stake progressively vests over time as the attestor remains active and in good standing. A portion of the stake unlocks at defined intervals (e.g. quarterly over two years). Unlocked tokens can be withdrawn or re-staked at the attestor's discretion.

This structure ensures that:

  • The attestor has immediate skin in the game from day one without purchasing tokens.
  • The grant cannot be immediately dumped on the market.
  • Long-term, active attestors are rewarded with progressively liquid stakes.
  • An attestor who deregisters early forfeits unvested stake, which returns to the grant pool.

6.3 Self-Funded Staking

Attestors who register after the grant pool is exhausted — or who wish to register at a higher stake than the grant provides — purchase and stake $NTC themselves. The minimum stake requirement remains the same regardless of funding source.

6.4 Slashing Conditions

  • Proven fraud: knowingly signing a false statement. Full slashing (both vested and unvested).
  • Negligence: failure to perform reasonable verification before signing. Partial slashing.
  • Key compromise without disclosure: failure to report compromise and initiate rotation within a reasonable period. Partial slashing.

6.5 Dispute Resolution

An affected party files an on-chain dispute against a specific attestation, posting a dispute bond in $NTC. The attestor's stake is frozen pending resolution. A cross-jurisdictional panel of other registered attestors reviews the evidence.

  • If upheld: the attestor's stake is slashed and distributed to the dispute filer and the panel. The dispute bond is returned.
  • If rejected: the dispute bond is slashed and distributed to the attestor and the panel. The attestor's stake is unfrozen.

The bond requirement prevents frivolous accusations. The cross-jurisdictional panel requirement prevents collusion within a single legal community.

6.6 Slashed Token Destination

Slashed tokens can be burned (permanent deflation) or redirected to the insurance pool (reimbursement), depending on the dispute type. The split ratio is a parameter of the dispute resolution mechanism and may vary.

7. Read-Fee Model

7.1 Attestations as Assets

Every time a protocol reads an attestation blob, they pay a read fee in $NTC. The fee splits three ways:

  1. The wallet holder — compensation for privacy exposure and incentive to notarise facts.
  2. The attestor — residual income for ongoing reputational and legal risk.
  3. The protocol burn — deflationary mechanism proportional to attestation demand.

7.2 Incentive Inversion

Without read fees, attestation is a cost borne reluctantly because some protocol requires it. With read fees, attestation is an investment — every boolean you notarise is a potential income stream. Wallet holders are incentivised to notarise every provable fact about themselves. Attestors build portfolios of signed attestations generating residual income in perpetuity.

7.3 The Privacy Tax

Each boolean is individually harmless. But booleans compose — enough together can fingerprint a person. The read fee makes individual queries cheap and rational while making bulk surveillance prohibitively expensive.

One insurer checking one boolean: $2.50. An adversary scraping 50 booleans across 10,000 wallets: $1.25 million. The economic model and the privacy model reinforce each other.

7.5 User Sovereignty

Wallet holders are warned that boolean intersections narrow anonymity sets. The protocol encourages distributing attestations across multiple wallets. Escalating read fees compensate for increased privacy exposure when multiple booleans on a single wallet are read.

7.6 Supply Impact

Read-fee burns scale with the installed base of attestations, not just the rate of new creation. A mature network with millions of existing booleans queried thousands of times daily generates substantial burn volume even if new attestation creation slows. The network becomes more deflationary as it matures.

8. Institutional Incentives

8.1 DAO Grants

The institutional grant pool incentivises licensing bodies, insurance companies, and other stakeholders to join the Notachain DAO. Registration as a DAO participant requires an institutional identity and a commitment to governance participation.

8.2 Referral Rewards

When an individual facilitates the on-chain registration of an institutional participant:

  • The institution receives a grant from the institutional pool.
  • The individual receives a referral reward.
  • The institution credits the individual for facilitating the registration.

This creates a network effect where participants are economically incentivised to recruit institutions, and institutions are economically incentivised to join because the grant is meaningful and the governance authority is real.
