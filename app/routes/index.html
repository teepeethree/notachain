The gap

The problem

There is no standardised way for a person to privately communicate verifiable facts about themselves to the blockchain. Oracles bring market data on-chain. Nothing brings human data on-chain — privately, verifiably, and with real-world accountability.

The solution

A credentialed legal professional verifies a fact about you and signs a boolean attestation on-chain. True or false. They face criminal consequences for lying. You face no identity exposure. The attestation lives forever, and you earn every time someone reads it.

The primitive
Every attestation reduces to a boolean

Not "this person earns $85,000." Just "this person meets the income threshold." True or false. Signed by a credentialed professional. Stored on IPFS. Referenced on-chain. Owned by you.

Some booleans, once true, are true forever. You only prove them once.

How it works
Two data types. Three contracts. That's the whole protocol.
01

Legal professionals register on-chain

A solicitor, notary, or barrister generates a keypair and registers on the Notachain registry — linked to their credentials, licensing body, and jurisdiction. They stake $PRZ tokens against their registration. They do this once.

02

You make a declaration

You visit a registered professional — the same way you'd make a statutory declaration today. You declare the facts you need attested. They verify your evidence and witness your declaration.

03

A boolean goes on-chain

The professional signs the attestation with their registered key. The proof document lives on IPFS — self-contained and content-addressed. Only the hash and the signature go on-chain. The boolean is now yours.

04

You earn every time it's read

Any protocol that needs to verify your boolean pays a read fee in $PRZ. The fee splits between you, the attestor, and a protocol burn. Your attestation is no longer a cost — it's a revenue-generating asset.

$PRZ token economics
Everyone earns. The network self-corrects.

The $PRZ token bootstraps the network, penalises fraud instantly, and creates a self-correcting supply mechanism that becomes more deflationary as the network matures.

Wallet holders

Earn from your own data

Every boolean you notarise earns $PRZ when protocols read it. The more useful your attestations, the more you earn. Your verified facts become income-generating assets.

Attestors

Residual income forever

Legal professionals earn a share of every read fee on every attestation they've ever signed. Plus grants for early registration. Plus staking rewards. The incentive to join is economic, not ideological.

The protocol

Self-correcting supply

An inverse sigmoid emission curve drives high rewards during growth, tapering as the network matures. Read fees and creation fees burn $PRZ proportionally to usage. The network becomes deflationary at scale.

Privacy by economics
The read fee is the privacy mechanism

Each boolean is individually harmless. But booleans compose — enough of them together can fingerprint a person. The $2.50 read fee makes individual queries cheap and rational. It makes bulk surveillance prohibitively expensive.

One insurer checking one boolean? $2.50. An adversary scraping 50 booleans across 10,000 wallets? $1.25 million. The economic model and the privacy model reinforce each other.

You control your exposure. Spread attestations across wallets. Choose which facts to notarise. The protocol provides the incentives and the tooling. The sovereignty is yours.

Two penalty timescales
Instant slashing. Then the law.

Every registered attestor stakes $PRZ against their registration. Fraudulent attestation triggers immediate slashing — economic loss in seconds. Then the legal system grinds forward: criminal prosecution, loss of license, unlimited personal liability. Two independent deterrents. Neither is sufficient alone. Together, they're overwhelming.

Trust model
Anchored to centuries-old legal infrastructure

Notachain doesn't invent a new trust system. It plugs into the one that already exists — 92 national notariats, every law society, every court commission. The accountability isn't tokenomic. It's criminal law.

Tier A

Civil law notaries

Government-appointed legal officers with quasi-judicial status. Their attestations are private judicial acts. 92 member notariats through the UINL, covering 70% of the global population.

Tier B

Common law lawyer-notaries

Solicitors, barristers, and commissioned notaries. Senior legal practitioners with professional liability, disciplinary oversight, and criminal exposure for facilitating false declarations.

Tier C

Administrative notaries

Non-lawyer notaries with more limited attestation authority. May require multiple attestations for high-value applications. Still carry legal accountability for fraudulent conduct.

First application
Wallet insurance that actually works

Crypto insurance fails because underwriters can't price anonymous risk. Notachain gives them the missing signal — attested off-chain facts combined with on-chain behavioral history. Accurate pricing. Real coverage. Claims adjudicated by reading the ledger, not arguing about what happened.

But insurance is just the first consumer. Any protocol that needs verified off-chain facts — lending, credit, identity, credentials, age-gating, procurement — can read Notachain attestations as a trust primitive.

Notachain is not a chain. It is the missing trust layer between the chain and the world.
