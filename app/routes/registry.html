Introduction

This document catalogues the licensing and governing bodies for notarial professionals across jurisdictions worldwide, for the purpose of establishing the Tier 1 registry of authorised licensing bodies under the Notachain Protocol.

Two primary legal traditions govern notarial practice globally:

  • Civil Law Notaries (Latin Notariat): highly credentialed, government-appointed legal officers with quasi-judicial status. Their authenticated instruments carry prima facie evidentiary weight in court. Coordinated internationally by the International Union of Notaries (UINL), comprising 92 member notariats representing approximately 70% of the global population.
  • Common Law Notaries: more limited administrative role, primarily identity verification and signature witnessing. Found in Australia, the UK, Canada (outside Quebec), and most US states. An important exception: England & Wales notaries are senior legal practitioners with significant authority for international documents.

Part 1: Civil Law Jurisdictions (UINL Members)

The UINL serves as the international umbrella body for civil law notariats. Member notariats are recognised as Tier 1 licensing bodies by virtue of UINL membership. The UINL itself is designated as a Meta-Tier 1 body.

United States

Approximately 4.5 million commissioned notaries — the largest number in any country — but administrative officials with limited authority compared to civil law notaries. Not lawyers and cannot provide legal advice. Notable exceptions: Louisiana (Napoleonic Code, expanded powers), Florida (civil law notary statute), Puerto Rico (UINL member, highest US notarial credential).

Part 3: Protocol Implementation Notes

Proposed Trust Tiers

Tier Category Trust Weight
Tier A UINL civil law notaries Highest. Quasi-judicial. Prima facie evidence. High-value underwriting.
Tier B Common law lawyer-notaries (AU, UK, NZ, SG, HK, Quebec) High. Senior practitioners with professional liability.
Tier C Common law non-lawyer notaries (most US, general commissioners) Moderate. May require multiple attestations above threshold values.

Key Rotation Authority

Key rotation requires a countersigned request from the Tier 1 licensing body via in-person statutory declaration. The on-chain record stores: old key (revoked), new key, licensing body countersigning address, rotation timestamp, and hash of the statutory declaration reference.

Ongoing Maintenance

The Notachain Protocol company is responsible for monitoring UINL membership changes, updating the Tier 1 registry on-chain, publishing changelogs, and processing community proposals for new Tier 1 body additions.
